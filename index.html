Odds and Ends

I am a PhD Candidate at Michigan State Unviersity, pursuing a dual degree in forestry and ecology.
My research looks to understand how climate, life history, and metabolomics interact to impact the productivity and mortality risk of trees throughout their lives. I am particularly interested in xylem architecture and it's role in tree hydraulics and carbon mobilization. Currently I am involved with in a number of projects supported by the Michigan DNR, US Forest Service, and the Ontario Ministry of Natural Resources trying to better understand how climate change stands to impact jack pine establishment, growth, and mortality in the Great Lakes region.
